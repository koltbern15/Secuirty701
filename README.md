# CompTIA Security+ (SY0-701) — Complete Study Course

A comprehensive, exam-focused study course for the **CompTIA Security+ SY0-701** exam: five full domain modules, quick-reference supplements, an Anki flashcard deck, and two self-contained dark-themed HTML apps — a full-length practice-exam simulator and a study dashboard.

Written for a working IT/security professional who wants depth without filler. All explanations and practice questions are original.

> ⚠️ **Verify before you book.** SY0-701 is the current version (launched November 2023, ~3-year cycle). Confirm the live objectives PDF at [CompTIA.org](https://www.comptia.org/certifications/security) — weights or objectives can shift.

## What's inside

All course files live in the `Security+ SY0-701/` folder.

**Study modules** — Markdown; open in [Obsidian](https://obsidian.md) or any Markdown reader. Internal `[[wikilinks]]` resolve when the folder is opened as (or inside) an Obsidian vault.

- `Security+ SY0-701 — Course Index.md` — **start here**: hub, weighted study plan, exam-day tactics
- `Domain 1 — General Security Concepts.md`
- `Domain 2 — Threats, Vulnerabilities & Mitigations.md`
- `Domain 3 — Security Architecture.md`
- `Domain 4 — Security Operations.md` — the deepest module (28% of the exam)
- `Domain 5 — Security Program Management & Oversight.md`

**Quick reference**

- `Acronym Master List.md` — every testable acronym, grouped by domain
- `Last-Week Cheat Sheet.md` — highest-yield facts for final review
- `Exam-Day One-Pager.md` — test-day tactics and a calm final checklist

**Practice & tracking** — open the `.html` files directly in a browser (no server or build step needed).

- `practice-exam-simulator.html` — 153-question original bank; each attempt samples 90 questions by domain weight, with a 90-minute timer, question navigator, multi-select support, per-domain scoring, and a PASS/FAIL verdict against 750
- `study-dashboard.html` — domain readiness tracker, weighted study-time planner, exam-date countdown, and a full objective checklist (progress saves locally in your browser)
- `flashcards.csv` — 300+ Anki-importable cards (header: `front,back,domain,tags`)

## How to use it

1. Open the folder in Obsidian and read the modules in order (1 → 5), spending the most time on **Domain 4** and **Domain 2** — together they're half the exam.
2. Take a **cold baseline run** in `practice-exam-simulator.html` to find your weak domains.
3. Track confidence and objectives in `study-dashboard.html`, then follow the weighted plan.
4. In the final week, drill the cheat sheet, one-pager, and flashcards.
